Baku, Azerbaijan

3 days in the Eurasian wonderland

For 3 days in Baku, what embraced us is kind of a paradox: a modern and urbanized city with ancient surroundings. Streets with luxury merchandises follow the line of shores against the Caspian Sea, Azerbaijanis wander over here and there enjoying the breeze from the world's largest lake. Thanks for the salt it has, the mild wind brings the slight taste of the real sea.

Lonely Planet dubbed Baku as "the architectural love child of Paris and Dubai". True, it shows many charms in modern buildings. The Heydar Aliyev Center represents the ingenuity of famous Iraqi-British architect Zaha Hadid: the gentle streamlines show mobility like the waving flags in the air with mysterious imagination, viewers find everything in peace during the whole observation.

In the Palace of the Shirvanshahs, described by UNESCO as "one of the pearls of Azerbaijan's architecture", it is easy to put yourself into historical region Shirvan during the 15th century. Try your best to be lost in the fantastic divanhane, the minaret, and mosques, surrounding with the ensemble of monuments, it is the chance to get a touch on symphony from Persia and Islamic world.

Along with the Palace of the Shirvanshahs, there stands an old tower, established in the 12th century, gazing at the Caspian Sea. Heavy wind with salinity brings the mottled cloth on the tower I guess, I would not make clear research to prove this hypothesis since I stopped my science classes after my secondary school. But the only thing I am pretty sure is, with the shimmering fragments of diamonds on the lake, the Maiden Tower looks like a fancy maiden standing on the land of Transcaucasia, waiting for something.

In the myth of the Maiden Tower, two versions are mostly spread into the whole world:

1丨Once upon the time, one poor guy met one noble girl in the old city, they were an item. Since they didn’t have equal social status, her father locked her in the tower. To show her uncompromising honesty, this stupid and cute girl suicided herself falling from the tower into the Caspian Sea. Tragically, the beloved guy, for the sake of love, committed the double suicide.

2丨Azerbaijan locates in the joint of two continents, and surely it was the favorite military place during the war period. The Maiden Tower protected Baku females’ virginities from the invaders in countless wars. That become so-called the Maiden Tower.

Again, I have no strengths to judge anyone within the romanticism and realism. The things left to me is to step into the tower enjoying its myth and magnificence. Up to the top, seven fire exits there represents the Zoroastrians’ elements because Zoroastrians believe 7 Steps to be in heaven.

Once I am on the top of the tower, the view collects the Flame Tower and the vast Caspian Sea together, of course in the aura with heavy wind. My minds drifted away with many imaginations about this magic country. Starting from the Sasanian Empire, when the feudal system reached the peak, the Byzantine Empire conquered and Saracen empire in 642 AD. Then. History never ends: Seljuk Empire and nomadism from Mongol Empire in the early 13th Century; Timurid Empire and fighting from Iran and Turkey; Safavid dynasty and being back into Persia; Russian Empire governance and the Soviet Union and independence. All the historical traces could be told in the lovely view.

We descended to the ground, the old city’s scene took me to reality. Alongside the little path, many art shops and booths welcomed us. Some are commercial business not attractive to us. They are just products under tourism. But some are incredible works. It is easy can tell the combination of Persian arts and Islamic arts into one piece, miniature from the master Tahir Salahov was astonishingly stunning among these artworks. Observing seriously, the detailed designs and layouts in color blew my mind under the sunshine of Baku.

Then we went to a local restaurant, which we found it was a Russian dinner place with global franchises. It started from a pot of pomegranate tea with elderflower, which is the typical one in Azerbaijan. The main course Tabaka chicken combined the Caucasian features to Turkish dish with 2 dipping sauces. The side dish was a small pot of pelmenis with lamb, the sour cream was the icing on the cake optimizing the mouthfeel: biting a little and the gravy bursts out. The most unforgettable one would be the dessert, the freshly baked crème brûlée. “So crispy on the top.” The vivid yellow popped up while digging into with a tiny spoon. I couldn’t help yelling how nice food the Azerbaijani have here!

Suddenly, a gust came and reminded me of the schedule; it was time to be off the next station. Hoped the time could be stopped over the moment, the classic Persian and Islamic scenery mingled with the unique taste of sea wind coming from the Caspian Sea, they were memorized and déjà vu come over and over again when I see the similar scene once more.

Lithuanian poet Albinas Žukauskas wrote in “The White Pianos”: Once, half a life ago I promised a good-looking affable young girl; A prima ballerina in Baku; Just for a single rapturous embrace; To forward her from Lithuania a snow-white piano; With rosy keys.… My youth has gone! Full long life has passed; But that rash promise - haunting as a dream - Is unredeemed as yet. Now I'd be willing to repay her. Unfortunately, in Lithuania there’s not a single White piano with a rosy keyboard left (How odd! At that time there were heaps of them!). I beg you all who have or saw one, let me know, I will be glad to overpay: It is my debt of honor after all – A sensible old age has to redeem; What senseless youth has ever promised.

Baku is always the city never to miss. Once you go there, everything taking place will be haunting I promise. No regrets are there at least.

Capsian Sea

World's largest lake, between Europe and Asia, to the east of the Caucasus Mountains and to the west of the steppe of Central Asia. And, it is salty...

Heydar Aliyev Center

signature landmark of modern Baku with its innovative and cutting-edge design: Fancy exhibition and a nice place to engage yourself.

Palace of the Shirvanshahs

One of the pearls of Azerbaijani architecture, a modern gaze in the 15th century on the land of Transcaucasia. From its perspective, just lost yourself in the interface from antiquity to modern era.

Pink Lake

This little-known lake in the suburb of Baku is not in the official tourism list. It is originally named as Binagadi Asphalt Lake/Lake Masazir/lake mirzaladi (Ambiguity exists because Masazir is not really pink in my experience but i noticed one really pink near the region)

Baku Eye

Not majestic but delicate. It shorten the distance gap between capsian sea and land, getting a closer touch to Baku's magic aura here, and for another thing, spinning ferris wheel causes mobilities in your distinguished trip.
